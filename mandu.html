Mandu Fort: The Forgotten Jewel of Madhya Pradesh

Explore the rich history and stunning architecture of Mandu Fort.

Historical Background of Mandu Fort

Mandu has a long history, dating back to the 6th century CE when it was ruled by the Paramara dynasty of Malwa. The fort reached its peak under the Malwa Sultanate (1401–1531 CE), particularly under Hoshang Shah and Mahmud Khilji. Later, it was taken over by the Mughals and eventually fell into decline.

One of the most famous legends associated with Mandu is the love story of Rani Roopmati and Baz Bahadur, the last independent ruler of Mandu. Their romance and tragic end add to the mystical charm of the fort.

Major Attractions Inside Mandu Fort

  • Jahaz Mahal (Ship Palace) - Built by Sultan Ghiyas-ud-din Khilji in the 15th century, designed to look like a floating ship.
  • Hindola Mahal (Swinging Palace) - Known for its sloping walls, served as a Durbar for the Malwa Sultans.
  • Rani Roopmati Pavilion - Offers breathtaking views of the Narmada River.
  • Baz Bahadur’s Palace - Famous for its acoustics and connected to Rani Roopmati Pavilion through a secret passage.
  • Hoshang Shah’s Tomb - India’s first marble tomb, built in 1440 CE.
  • Jami Masjid - A massive mosque inspired by the mosques of Damascus.
  • Nilkanth Mahal - Originally a Shiva temple, later converted into a pleasure retreat.
  • Rewa Kund - A sacred water reservoir built for Rani Roopmati’s pavilion.

Best Time to Visit Mandu Fort

Monsoon (July – September) – The fort looks magical with lush greenery and mist.

Winter (October – February) – Best weather for exploring the fort.

Avoid Summers (March – June) – Very hot, with temperatures reaching 40°C.

How to Reach Mandu Fort?

By Air: Nearest airport is Devi Ahilya Bai Holkar Airport, Indore (100 km away).

By Train: Nearest railway stations are Ratlam or Indore. From there, take a bus or taxi to Mandu.

By Road: Well-connected by roads to Indore (100 km) and Dhar (35 km). Buses and taxis are available from nearby cities.

Legends of Mandu – The Story of Rani Roopmati & Baz Bahadur

The most famous tale of Mandu is the tragic love story of Baz Bahadur and Rani Roopmati. Baz Bahadur, the ruler of Malwa, fell in love with Rani Roopmati, a beautiful singer. He built Rani Roopmati’s Pavilion so she could see the Narmada River daily. However, the Mughal emperor Akbar’s general, Adham Khan, attacked Mandu. Baz Bahadur fled, and to avoid capture, Rani Roopmati poisoned herself. This tragic love story adds a mystical and romantic aura to Mandu Fort.

Why Visit Mandu Fort?

  • Rich Historical Legacy – A blend of Hindu, Afghan, and Mughal architecture.
  • Breathtaking Views – Surrounded by green hills, lakes, and rivers.
  • Romantic & Tragic Tales – The legend of Rani Roopmati and Baz Bahadur.
  • Perfect Monsoon Destination – One of the best places to visit during the rains.
