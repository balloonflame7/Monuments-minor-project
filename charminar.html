Historical Background

The Charminar was built in 1591 by Sultan Muhammad Quli Qutb Shah, the founder of Hyderabad. It marked the end of a deadly plague in the region. The Sultan is believed to have prayed for the end of the epidemic and vowed to build a mosque if his prayers were answered. Charminar was constructed at the center of the new city (Hyderabad), symbolizing the cityâ€™s founding moment.

Legacy

Charminar is an enduring symbol of Hyderabad's culture, architecture, and pluralism. It has been featured in movies, stamps, coins, and the logo of Telangana tourism. It has also been declared a protected monument by the Archaeological Survey of India (ASI).
