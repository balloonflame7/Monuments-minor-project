Introduction

The Parliament of India is the supreme legislative body of the Republic of India. It symbolizes India's democracy and heritage. With the shift to a new Parliament building in 2023, the institution now blends historical legacy with modern design and technology.

  • Architects: Sir Edwin Lutyens & Sir Herbert Baker
  • Shape: Circular (inspired by the Ashoka Chakra)
  • Material: Sandstone
  • Location: Sansad Marg, New Delhi

  • Diameter: 173 meters
  • Columns: 144 sandstone columns
  • Three Major Chambers:
    • Lok Sabha (Lower House)
    • Rajya Sabha (Upper House)
    • Central Hall (used for joint sessions and major events)
  • Blend of British classical design with Indian elements (e.g., chhatris)
