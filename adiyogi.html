Basic Information

Name Adiyogi – The Abode of Yoga

Height 112 feet (34.3 meters)
Material Steel
Designed By Sadhguru Jaggi Vasudev and the Isha Foundation
Represents Lord Shiva as the first yogi (Adiyogi)
Inaugurated 24 February 2017 by Prime Minister Narendra Modi
Recognition Featured in the Guinness Book of World Records for the world's largest bust sculpture

Spiritual Significance

Adiyogi means “the first yogi.” He is considered the originator of yoga, who shared 112 methods of attaining enlightenment. The 112-foot height symbolizes these 112 possibilities for human transformation. Adiyogi is not seen as a religious idol but a symbol of inner well-being and spirituality beyond religion.

Architecture & Design

Element

  • Material: Made from steel; weighs around 500 tonnes
  • Design: A black bust statue of Lord Shiva, with a peaceful yet intense expression
  • Surroundings: Set against the Velliangiri Hills; offers a serene and powerful ambiance
  • Yogic Touches: Features like the open chest, long hair, and third eye reflect yogic depth

Interesting Facts

  • The statue is a symbol, not a deity worshipped in traditional ways.
  • It is located at the Isha Yoga Center, a hub for yogic practices and meditation.
  • Every year, Mahashivratri is celebrated here with music, dance, meditations, and lakhs of devotees.
  • The face of Adiyogi has been replicated in Chikkaballapur, Karnataka, as a full-body form of Adiyogi.

Isha Foundation Activities

Sadhguru, founder of Isha Foundation, created Adiyogi as a conscious response to modern-day stress and mental suffering. The site promotes:

  • Inner Engineering programs
  • Surya Kriya & Yogasanas
  • Free meditative spaces like the Dhyanalinga
