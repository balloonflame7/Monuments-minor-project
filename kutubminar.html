Qutub Minar – A Marvel of Indo-Islamic Architecture

A UNESCO World Heritage Site and a symbol of India's rich history.

Quick Facts

Feature Detail
Location Mehrauli, Delhi, India
Construction Started 1192 AD
Built By Qutb-ud-din Aibak (started), Iltutmish (completed)
Materials Red sandstone, marble
Height 72.5 meters (238 feet)
Floors 5 distinct storeys
Style Indo-Islamic architecture
UNESCO Status World Heritage Site (1993)

Historical Background

Qutb-ud-din Aibak, the founder of the Delhi Sultanate, started construction in 1192 to celebrate Muslim dominance after defeating the last Hindu kingdom of Delhi. Only the first storey was completed by Aibak. His son-in-law and successor Iltutmish added three more storeys in 1220. The fifth storey was added by Firoz Shah Tughlaq in the 14th century after restoration due to lightning damage. The minaret is named after Qutb-ud-din Aibak, though some believe it's also in honour of Qutubuddin Bakhtiyar Kaki, a famous Sufi saint.

Architecture and Design

Feature

  • Materials Used: Red sandstone (1st–3rd floors), white marble (4th–5th floors)
  • Shape: Tapering cylindrical tower – base is 14.3 m wide, top is 2.7 m wide
  • Storeys: 5 – each with projecting balconies (jharokhas)
  • Calligraphy: Verses from the Qur'an engraved on walls, decorative carvings
  • Style: A blend of Afghan and Persian Islamic style with local Hindu motifs
  • Decoration: Lotus motifs, Arabic inscriptions, intricate carvings

Inscriptions and Restoration

Inscriptions in Arabic and Nagari script describe the history of Qutub Minar. The tower has been damaged multiple times due to lightning and earthquakes. Restorations were carried out by:

  • Firoz Shah Tughlaq
  • Sikander Lodi
  • The British, in the 19th century

Surrounding Monuments (Qutb Complex)

  • Quwwat-ul-Islam Mosque: The first mosque in India, built using materials from 27 Hindu and Jain temples.
  • Iron Pillar of Delhi: A 7.2 m tall iron pillar over 1600 years old that does not rust, believed to be from the time of Chandragupta II (4th century).
  • Alai Minar: An incomplete tower started by Alauddin Khilji meant to be twice as tall as Qutub Minar.
  • Tombs of Iltutmish & Alauddin Khilji

Significance

Qutub Minar is a symbol of Islamic rule in North India and represents the fusion of Islamic and Hindu architecture. It is a major tourist attraction and subject of global academic interest, standing as a UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1993.

Interesting Facts

  • Qutub Minar is the tallest brick minaret in the world.
  • A lightning conductor was installed by the British in 1829.
  • You can no longer climb the tower since 1981 due to a tragic accident.
  • Inspired later constructions like the Chand Minar in Maharashtra.
