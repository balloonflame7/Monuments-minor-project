Khajuraho Temples

A Detailed Overview

Historical Background

The Khajuraho Group of Monuments is one of India’s most famous temple complexes, known for its stunning sculptural art, intricate carvings, and exquisite Nagara-style architecture. Located in Chhatarpur district, Madhya Pradesh, these temples were built between 950 and 1050 AD during the rule of the Chandela dynasty.

The name Khajuraho is derived from the Sanskrit word "Khajura" (dates or palm trees), possibly referring to the region's once-abundant date palm trees.

Architectural Features

1. Temple Layout & Design

Each temple follows a typical five-part structure, which includes:

  • Garbhagriha – The inner sanctum housing the deity.
  • Mandapa – The entrance hall.
  • Ardhamandapa – The porch or secondary hall.
  • Mahamandapa – The main hall for gatherings.
  • Shikhara – The towering spire over the sanctum.

The temples are built using sandstone without mortar, using a locking system that holds the stones together.

2. Intricate Carvings & Sculptures

The walls of the temples are adorned with intricate carvings, showcasing:

  • Mythological tales from Hindu epics (Ramayana & Mahabharata).
  • Celestial beings (Apsaras) & divine figures.
  • Musicians, dancers, and daily life scenes.
  • Erotic sculptures depicting Kama (desire) from Kamasutra.

The erotic carvings make up only about 10% of the total sculptures, symbolizing the Tantric traditions of that time.

Classification of the Temples

1. Western Group (Most Famous & Grandest)

These temples are the most well-preserved and attract the majority of tourists. Notable temples include:

  • Kandariya Mahadev Temple – The largest and most ornate temple dedicated to Lord Shiva.
  • Lakshmana Temple – Dedicated to Vishnu, known for its elaborate sculptures.
  • Vishwanath Temple – Features a majestic Nandi bull statue.
  • Chausath Yogini Temple – The oldest temple, dedicated to 64 Yoginis.

2. Eastern Group (Mainly Jain Temples)

Includes:

  • Parshvanath Temple – The largest Jain temple in Khajuraho.
  • Adinath Temple – Dedicated to the first Jain Tirthankara.
  • Brahma Temple – Features a four-faced Lingam.

3. Southern Group (Least Known but Beautiful)

Includes:

  • Duladeo Temple – Dedicated to Shiva, known for its finely detailed carvings.
  • Chaturbhuj Temple – The only temple without erotic sculptures, housing a large Vishnu idol.

Religious & Cultural Significance

The temples reflect a blend of Hinduism and Jainism, showcasing religious harmony. The sculptures depict Dharma (duty), Artha (wealth), Kama (desire), and Moksha (liberation), representing the complete human life cycle. The erotic art symbolizes the connection between divine and human love and the belief that spiritual enlightenment is achieved through worldly experiences.

Decline & Rediscovery

After the 13th century, Khajuraho declined due to the fall of the Chandela dynasty and invasions by Muslim rulers. The temples were abandoned and covered by dense forests for centuries. They were rediscovered in 1838 by Captain T.S. Burt, a British officer.

Modern Importance & Tourism

Declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1986, the temples host the Khajuraho Dance Festival every February, showcasing Indian classical dance forms. They are a major tourist attraction, drawing visitors from around the world.
