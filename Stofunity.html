Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel, known as the Iron Man of India, was the first Deputy Prime Minister and Home Minister of independent India. He played a crucial role in the integration of over 560 princely states into the Indian Union and believed in national unity, discipline, and strong administration.

The Statue of Unity is the tallest statue in the world.

Design & Structure

The statue depicts Sardar Patel in a standing pose, dressed in traditional Indian attire, facing the Sardar Sarovar Dam on the Narmada River. It is designed to withstand wind resistance up to 180 km/h and earthquake resistance up to 6.5 on the Richter scale. A viewing gallery at 153 meters accommodates 200 people and offers a panoramic view.

Materials Used

  • 1,700 tonnes of bronze
  • 18,500 tonnes of reinforced steel
  • 210,000 cubic meters of cement concrete
  • Iron contribution campaign: farmers across India donated iron tools for the base.

  • The statue's height (182 m) matches the 182 seats in the Gujarat Legislative Assembly.
  • Built in just 33 months, a record for such a large structure.
  • Can be seen from 7 kilometers away.
  • The statue changes color with sunlight and weather due to the bronze alloy.
