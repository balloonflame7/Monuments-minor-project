A sacred site believed to be the birthplace of Lord Rama.

Historical Significance

Ayodhya is believed to be the birthplace of Lord Rama, the 7th avatar of Vishnu. The original Ram temple is believed to have existed at the Janmabhoomi site but was destroyed in the 16th century.

Babri Masjid Controversy

In 1528, Babri Masjid was built by Mughal general Mir Baqi, allegedly over the ruins of a temple. This led to centuries of conflict, lawsuits, and a long-standing demand for the reconstruction of the temple. In December 1992, Babri Masjid was demolished during a mass movement.

Legal and Supreme Court Verdict

On 9 November 2019, the Supreme Court of India gave a unanimous verdict: the disputed land would be given to Hindus for building Ram Mandir, and a separate 5-acre land would be given to Muslims to build a mosque elsewhere in Ayodhya.

Architecture & Design

Feature

  • Style: Nagara style (traditional North Indian temple architecture)
  • Material: Bansi Paharpur pink sandstone (no iron or steel used)
  • Foundation: Interlocking stones with a 14-meter concrete base
  • Orientation: East-facing temple with Surya Darshan at sunrise
  • Mandap Design: 5 Mandaps – Nritya, Rang, Sabha, Kirtan, Prarthana Mandap
  • Floors: 3 floors, 392 pillars, 44 doors
  • Decorations: Carvings of gods, sages, animals, flowers

Main Features

  • Garbhagriha (Sanctum Sanctorum): Houses the idol of Ram Lalla (child form of Shri Ram), in black stone, shown standing on a lotus base.
  • Ram Darbar: Will include idols of Lord Ram, Sita, Lakshman, and Hanuman.
  • Mandapams: Each one is artistically carved and supported by detailed stone pillars.
  • Park & Landscape: Beautiful gardens, museum, research centre, and pilgrim facilities.
  • Ram Path: Grand entrance path with 3D murals, sculptures, and history panels.

Management and Organization

The temple is being constructed and managed by Shri Ram Janmabhoomi Teerth Kshetra Trust, formed by the Indian government. Over ₹3,500 crore has been collected via public donations. Temple construction involves experts from IITs, ISRO, L&T, Tata, CBRI, etc.

Pran Pratishtha (22 January 2024)

Performed by Prime Minister Narendra Modi and Ram Janmbhoomi priests, the idol of Ram Lalla will be consecrated with Vedic rituals, watched by millions across the globe via live broadcasts.

Religious Importance

One of the most sacred pilgrimage sites for Hindus, representing the victory of Dharma (righteousness) and a symbol of faith, unity, and national pride.

Interesting Facts

  • The height of the temple is 161 feet, taller than many historic temples.
  • The Ram Lalla idol is 51 inches tall and weighs around 1.5 tons.
  • Solar and green technologies are being used in temple design.
  • The temple will host millions of devotees every year.
  • A Ramayana Museum and Vedic Research Center are also being built.
