Introduction

India Gate is a war memorial located in New Delhi, India. It was built to honor the soldiers of the British Indian Army who lost their lives in World War I and the Third Anglo-Afghan War. The monument is one of the most iconic landmarks in India and a symbol of national pride.

Historical Background

The foundation stone was laid on February 10, 1921, by the Duke of Connaught. It was inaugurated on February 12, 1931, by Lord Irwin, the then Viceroy of India. The memorial is dedicated to the 84,000 soldiers of the British Indian Army who died in World War I (1914â€“1918) and the Third Anglo-Afghan War (1919). The names of 13,300 soldiers are inscribed on its walls.

Architecture and Design

Designed by Sir Edwin Lutyens, the architect behind New Delhi's layout, the monument follows classical and Indo-Saracenic architectural styles. Made from yellow and red sandstone, it gives a majestic look. The archway is 42 meters tall, and the entire structure resembles the Arc de Triomphe in Paris.

Significance & Present-Day Importance

India Gate is a major tourist attraction and a site for national events like Republic Day. The monument symbolizes sacrifice, bravery, and patriotism. Every year, the Prime Minister pays tribute on Republic Day (January 26).
