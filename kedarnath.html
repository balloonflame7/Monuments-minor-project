Kedarnath Temple – The Abode of Lord Shiva

A sacred pilgrimage site nestled in the Himalayas.

Basic Information

Feature Detail
Location Kedarnath, Uttarakhand, India (at 3,583 m)
Deity Worshipped Lord Shiva (Kedareshwar form)
Built By Believed to be built by Pandavas, later restored by Adi Shankaracharya
Age Over 1,200 years old (ancient origin)
Architecture Style North Indian Himalayan stone architecture
Material Massive grey stone slabs
Pilgrimage Circuit One of the 12 Jyotirlingas and Char Dham of Uttarakhand

Spiritual & Mythological Significance

According to legend, after the Kurukshetra war, the Pandavas sought forgiveness from Lord Shiva. Shiva eluded them and took refuge in the Himalayas as a bull (Nandi form). When found, he dived into the earth, and parts of his body appeared in different locations:

  • Kedarnath (hump)
  • Tungnath (arms)
  • Rudranath (face)
  • Madhyamaheshwar (navel)
  • Kalpeshwar (hair)

Together, these are known as the Panch Kedar.

Architecture of Kedarnath Temple

Feature

  • Style: Stone slab-based, ancient North Indian Nagara architecture
  • Material: Constructed using interlocking stones, no mortar used
  • Climate Resistance: Stands strong even in heavy snowfall and earthquakes
  • Special Design: Structure absorbs minor shocks—helpful in earthquake zones
  • Interior: Contains a conical rock structure (lingam) as the main deity
  • Rituals: Morning: Maha Abhishek

Important Events

  • Restored by Adi Shankaracharya in the 8th century to revive Sanatan Dharma.
  • 2013 Flash Floods: Massive destruction, but the temple miraculously survived.
  • A huge rock (Bhim Shila) behind the temple protected it from the flood waters.
  • Post-2013: Major reconstruction and safety enhancements by the Indian government.

How to Reach Kedarnath

  • Nearest town: Gaurikund
  • Trek: 16 km uphill trek from Gaurikund to Kedarnath Temple
  • Helicopter service: Available from places like Phata and Guptkashi

Visual Highlights

  • Snow-covered temple with Himalayan backdrop
  • Bhim Shila stone behind the temple
  • Pilgrims and sadhus on Char Dham Yatra
  • Night-time aarti with lamps and chants
